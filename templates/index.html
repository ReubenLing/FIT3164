Water Management in Dairy Farms

water_drop

The dairy industry is the highest consumer of water in Australia. As the sea level rises, so too does our urgency to build a sustainable society where our children's children still have forests to protect and water to drink.
As a third of irrigated land in Australia is occupied by dairy farms, dairy farmers hold a lot of power to make a positive change for our country and conserve our life-giving resources.

This project aims to use data visualisation to inform dairy farm owners and managers about the biggest culprits of on-farm water usage and how to reduce the impact of them.

Irrigation

The majority of dairy farms employ irrigation on their fields, ensuring the plants grow healthily to feed the grazing cows.

The water this irrigation uses is highly dependent on the plants being grown. Ensure that the plants being grown are suited to your area and climate, and naturally require less water. (Rogers et al., 2017)

The type and implementation of these irrigation systems also impacts water usage, so it is important to perform proper maintenance and select an irrigation system that works for your farm's requirements.

Cow Consumption

A major component of dairy farm water usage goes directly to the cows in the form of drinking water.

In addition, the proportion of dry matter, protein, and sodium that the cows are fed (Schroeder, 2015) can slightly increase water consumption.
So while you can't change the temperature, you can prevent unnecessary water consumption by optimising these nutrients in manufactured feed to minimise water usage, while maintaining the health of the animals.

Washing and Cleaning

Water must be allocated to washing sheds and equipment, cooling milk, and waste flushing systems. The water used in these processes is heavily influenced by the milking systems employed.
Robotic or automated milking systems may be more water efficient (Thomson & Corscadden, 2018), as these systems can more accurately measure the amount of water needed for each wash (Nagyp√°l et al., 2020). Alternatively, you could potentially achieve similar results by training employees on applying the optimal amount of water through hoses and other equipment.

Runoff

Runoff wastewater from farms can have a huge impact on local waterways. Proper management of wastewater is the difference between destroying ecosystems or creating new ones.

Aim to capture and manage all effluent via traps and sumps. Prevent solids from mixing with water and store effluents in such a way that they do not leech into water sources. (DairyGains)

Recycle and reuse water. Wastewater treatment technology ranges from mechanical, chemical, and bacterial filters, to constructed wetlands (sources).

This was a brief introduction to a few ways in which you can reduce the water footprint of a dairy farm. The volume of new technology and research on reducing water consumption is ever-increasing, so there are many options to find what is best for your business, and your animals.
